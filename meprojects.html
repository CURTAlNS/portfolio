Motor Encoder Classwork

This project involved designing and implementing a closed-loop motor monitoring and control system using an Arduino Uno, a DC motor with a rotary encoder, and an HW-95 motor driver module. The goal was to accurately determine the angular position, rotational speed (RPM), and linear speed while maintaining reliable control over motor direction and rotation.

The rotary encoder attached to the motor shaft provided pulse signals corresponding to incremental shaft movement. These pulses were read by the Arduino using interrupt-based counting to ensure accurate measurement even at higher speeds. By tracking the number of encoder ticks over time, the system computed the angular displacement and rotational velocity of the motor. The encoder resolution allowed the program to convert pulse counts into precise angular position measurements and calculate RPM by measuring the pulse frequency within a defined sampling interval.

4 Bar Crank

This project involved analyzing the motion and loading characteristics of a four-bar crank mechanism using a CAD motion simulation. The system consisted of a rotating crank connected to a set of links that converted rotational motion into complex planar movement. A motion study was created to simulate the mechanism operating at a constant angular speed of 600 RPM.

From the motion study, key performance metrics were determined including the maximum motor torque, the crank angle at which this torque occurs, the maximum vertical forces acting on the bearing supports, and the minimum motor power required to maintain a constant rotational speed. The analysis also examined periods of negative motor torque, which indicate instances where the inertia and gravitational forces of the links assist the motion of the mechanism rather than resisting it.
